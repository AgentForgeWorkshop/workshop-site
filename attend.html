FAQ

Good to know.

Do I need prior experience with AI agents? +

No. AgentForge is built specifically for domain scientists who haven't yet integrated agents into their work. Tutorials are ordered from foundations to advanced topics so everyone can follow the progression. Comfort with basic scripting helps for the hands-on sessions.

What will I leave with? +

A working prototype of a personal AI research assistant, a shared GitHub repository, and a network of collaborators. Teams also produce a short report of insights and open questions, which feeds into a post-event perspective article.

What should I bring to the hackathon? +

Your own tools, datasets, and a real research bottleneck. Teams of 3–5 form around shared scientific disciplines, then design, prompt, and deploy a custom agent over two days.

Who can apply? +

Schmidt AI in Science Fellows and alumni, plus external participants from universities. We look for demonstrated interest in — or relevant expertise related to — the workshop themes, across a diverse range of disciplines.

Are the sessions recorded? +

Yes — tutorial recordings are planned for upload to YouTube after the event, and all notebooks live in a dedicated public GitHub repository.

Who supports the workshop? +

AgentForge is supported by Schmidt Sciences through the AI in Science Fellowship Community Initiatives Fund, and endorsed by NTU's Research Support Office.
